Mirdek: a card cipher inspired by "Solitaire"

Problems with Bruce Schneier's "Solitaire" describes a bias in the output of the card cipher "Solitaire". Here I'll try and describe an alternative cipher of my own design, "Mirdek", that avoids these problems.

About Mirdek

Mirdek is intended to fulfill exactly the same goals as Bruce Schneier's card cipher Solitaire: a cipher that can be operated entirely by hand, but that resists machine cryptanalysis even from very well-funded adversaries. See John Savard's proposal for another example.

Strengths

  • No Solitaire-like bias
  • Use of IV drawn from shuffling means passphrase can be used many times
  • Very easy operation by comparison to other proposals - no mental arithmetic is required, only card counting and matching
  • Relatively fast - practical even for short messages
  • Easy to learn
  • No jokers are needed

Weaknesses

  • The cipher is very vulnerable to a side channel attack using a listening device
  • Distinctive deck arrangement must be hidden if the use of encryption is to be kept secret.
  • IV makes ciphertext 26 characters longer than plaintext
  • Plaintext feedback (rather than pure RNG operation) makes analysis harder
  • State space limited to (26!)^2 ~= 2^177 rather than Solitaire's 54! ~= 2^237
  • New and unproven
